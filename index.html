What is general aviation?

General aviation are all civilian aviation operations other than scheduled airline transport. Examples can be flight training, private flights or just casual flying in your own or rented aircraft. According to AOPA more than 90% of all civil aircraft registered in the United States are general aviation aircraft.

Licensing

There are 3 kinds of airplane pilot licenses:

  1. PPL(A)
  2. CPL(A)
  3. ATPL(A)

PPL(A) is a basic license that is the first step into aviation. With this license you are not allowed to earn money on your operations, however you can share costs of the flight with your passangers. By default you can fly any single engine piston with MTOW up to 5700 kg but this can be enhanced by type ratings and other additional certification.
Requirements to get it are minimum age of 16 at the start of the training and 17 at the moment of applying for the license and 2nd class medical certificate.

CPL(A) allows a pilot to earn money on aviation operations, but not in a regular airline transport. You may for example charge passengers for scenic flights.
Requirements to get it are valid PPL(A) license, 1st class medical certificate, at least 150 hours of flight experience 10 of which as PIC, MEP(L) and VFR Night certification as well as a completed ATPL(A) or CPL(A) theoretical course.

ATPL(A) shares the practical part with CPL(A) but has a way more advanced theoretical training. This license allows you to fly as an airline pilot in regular commercial transport.
Requirements to get it are 1st class medical certificate, ATPL(A) Frozen license and MCC+JOC certification.
